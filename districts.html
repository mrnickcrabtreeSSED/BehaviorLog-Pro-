The problem

The FBA is defensible. The data behind it usually isn't.

Districts don't lose due-process cases because their behaviorists are wrong. They lose them because the observation record was reconstructed, undated, or sitting in a spreadsheet nobody can authenticate.

Compliance exposure

Discovery finds the gaps

A BIP without contemporaneous ABC data behind it is the first thing opposing counsel pulls apart. Clipboards and end-of-week recollection don't hold up — dated, per-event records do.

Stretched specialists

Your BCBAs cover five schools

Every hour a behaviorist spends chasing paper data or re-teaching a tally system is an hour of clinical capacity your district already paid for and didn't get.

Vendor sprawl

Behavior data lives everywhere

Point sheets here, a consumer spreadsheet there, a binder in the psych's car. No isolation between schools, no audit trail, and no answer when a parent asks who has seen their child's record.

AI, with guardrails

Drafts the documents. Never sees a name.

BehaviorLog Pro can draft a BIP, FBA summary, or measurable IEP goals directly from the observation record — using your district's own templates as the structural guide.

1

De-identify first

Before anything leaves the app, the student's and staff names are stripped and replaced with placeholders. Only de-identified behavioral data is processed.

2

Draft from real data

The draft is built from the actual sessions — frequencies, durations, antecedent patterns, hypotheses — not from boilerplate.

3

Restore locally

The student's name is substituted back on the specialist's own device. The AI provider never received it.

4

Clinician signs off

Every output is a draft. It is reviewed, edited, and approved by your licensed behaviorist or school psychologist before it goes near an IEP.

District choice, in writing. AI drafting is enabled per-district under your data privacy agreement — and the de-identification above is documented in our privacy policy, not just in this brochure.

The fine print, up front

What we'd want to know if we were you.

Who is behind this?

Sure Step Education — founded by working special-education practitioners. BehaviorLog Pro is built and supported by the founders; when you email, a founder answers. DailyWins, our goal-progress tracker for non-public schools, is the sister product.

What does rollout actually take?

It's a web app (installable on phones and tablets — no app store). We create your district tenant; your district admin sends role-based invite links; staff sign in with Google or email. A school can be live the same afternoon.

Can we leave?

Yes, cleanly. Your data exports in machine-readable JSON at any time, and on contract end all pupil records are returned and then deleted within 60 days, with written certification.

What about the AI features — can we turn them off?

AI drafting is a district-level decision made under your DPA. With it off, everything else works identically. With it on, names are stripped before processing and every draft requires clinician review.

Is this a student-facing app?

No. Students never log in and never enter data. All records are created by your authorized staff, which keeps the COPPA surface simple and the FERPA chain of custody clean.

What's genuinely new here vs. a spreadsheet?

Three things a spreadsheet can't do: per-event timestamps captured in the moment (contemporaneity you can defend), database-enforced access scoping (who-saw-what you can answer), and clinical measurement flows (latency chains, defined intensity scales) that produce data a BCBA can actually base a function hypothesis on.
