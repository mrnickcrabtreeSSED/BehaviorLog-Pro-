The problem

The FBA is defensible.
The data behind it usually isn't.

Reconstructed records

Point sheets and recollection get reverse-engineered into progress data the week it's due. Contemporaneity — the thing hearings turn on — is exactly what's missing.

Specialists stretched thin

BCBAs and school psychs covering multiple schools spend clinical hours collecting and transcribing instead of analyzing and writing.

No chain of custody

Spreadsheets and binders can't answer the questions a program review asks: who recorded this, when, and who has seen it since?

BehaviorLog Pro05
For your clinicians

Measurement a BCBA can build a hypothesis on.

Latency → duration chains

The directive is required before the latency timer can start; when the behavior is observed, one tap logs the latency and rolls straight into the duration window. One incident, clinically complete.

Operational definitions everywhere

Intensity is a 1–4 scale in the specialist's own written definitions — and it stays locked until those definitions exist. Behavior targets carry their op-defs to every teacher who uses them.

Function screening built in

QABF and FAST administered, sent, and scored in-platform; an FBA-readiness gauge shows when the observation record can support the write-up.

Role-gated by design

Latency and intensity simply don't exist for non-clinical roles. Teachers collect what teachers should collect.

BehaviorLog Pro07
Student privacy

The policy says what the database enforces.

FERPA — school-official model

The district owns all pupil records; Sure Step operates under 34 CFR § 99.31(a)(1) with CSDPA / SDPC data privacy agreements supported.

SOPIPA — clean

No advertising, no profiling, no sale of student data, ever. Deletion on request. 30-day written notice for material policy changes.

Security posture

Row-level security on every table · TLS 1.2+ in transit · AES-256 at rest · US-region hosting · bcrypt-hashed credentials · API keys server-side only.

COPPA — simple by design

Students never log in and never enter data; all records are created by authorized staff.

Read it yourself: the public privacy policy documents even our support-access ("break-glass") procedure and its audit trail.
